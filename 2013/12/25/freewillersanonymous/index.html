  9. We will make direct amends to such people wherever possible, except when to do so would injure them or others. (If one is in accord with all the other steps and feels amends necessary, this would happen naturally). (Right Action)

Merely realizing that free will is a myth doesn‚Äôt purge the instinct that drives our deepest sense of it. Now, 30 years later, I have found no evidence for free will. All I see are biological forces directing the choices I make. Poof went my belief in free will, and yet feelings arise at times that suggest I still believe in free will. I now finally realize that it doesn‚Äôt matter what we believe or don‚Äôt believe‚Äîinstinct rules us in the end. We can‚Äôt help it; it is innate. However, we can come to recognize this instinct‚Äôs influence over our lives. We can be recovering free-willers.
